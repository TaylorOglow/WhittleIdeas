About

A few words about who writes here.

I follow geopolitics the way some people follow sport — obsessively, with opinions, and occasional heartbreak. I also train regularly, sit in meditation most mornings, and spend my weekends with a knife and a block of wood.

This blog is where those four worlds get equal time. I write to think things through, not to perform expertise. You'll find analysis alongside training logs, carving notes next to essays on statecraft.

Glad you're here.

Recommendations

International Relations

The Clash of Civilizations and the Remaking of World Order

Samuel P. Huntington

Still worth arguing with. Flawed but generative — it forces you to think harder about cultural fault lines in global politics.

Fitness

Practical Programming for Strength Training

Mark Rippetoe & Andy Baker

Clear-eyed about how adaptation works over time. The best resource I've found for understanding why programming decisions actually matter.

Meditation

The Mind Illuminated

Culadasa (John Yates)

A rigorous map of meditative development that finally gave my practice a coherent framework. Dense but worth every page.

Wood Carving

The Woodwright's Shop

Roy Underhill

Underhill's philosophy of hand-tool work changed how I think about making things. Warm, precise, and deeply practical.

International Relations

Prisoners of Geography

Tim Marshall

A refreshingly physical account of world affairs — how mountains, rivers, and coastlines shape the decisions of states.

Meditation

Waking Up

Sam Harris

A secular approach to contemplative practice. Best suited to those who find traditional frameworks a barrier to starting.

International Relations

The Tragedy of Great Power Politics: A Primer

John Mearsheimer — Foreign Affairs

A concise statement of offensive realism. Whether you agree or not, this framework clarifies your thinking about state behaviour.

Fitness

The Case for Training for Longevity, Not Performance

Peter Attia — outlive.com

Reframed how I think about the purpose of training. The "Centenarian Decathlon" framing is now part of how I programme my own work.

Meditation

Mastering the Core Teachings of the Buddha

Daniel Ingram — free PDF

Controversial and unflinching. Ingram insists on treating meditation as a skill with measurable outcomes. Even skeptics benefit from the challenge.

Wood Carving

Why Carve? The Case for Making Useless Things Well

Barn the Spoon — barnthespoon.com

A philosophical defence of craft that resonated with me deeply. Short, honest, and surprisingly moving.

International Relations

This Is Not a Crisis, It's a Transition

Adam Tooze — Chartbook

Tooze's newsletter consistently offers the most rigorous structural analysis of global economic politics available in essay form.

Fitness

The Specificity Trap: Why Training Hard Isn't Enough

Chris Beardsley — medium.com

One of the clearest explanations of how specificity and variation interact in strength programming.
